Suicide is the third leading cause of death among people ages 15-24

Know the Signs

  • Change in eating and sleeping habits
  • Withdrawal from friends, family, and regular activities
  • Violent actions, rebellious behavior, or running away
  • Drug and alcohol use
  • Unusual neglect of personal appearance
  • Marked personality change
  • Persistent boredom, difficulty concentrating, or a decline in the quality of schoolwork
  • Frequent complaints about physical symptoms, often related to emotions, such as stomachaches, headaches, fatigue, etc.
  • Loss of interest in pleasurable activites
  • Not tolerating praise or awards

How to Help

  • Ask the person if they re doing ok, feeling depressed, or thinking about suicide
  • Don't be afraid to mention suicide, it won't put any ideas in their head that aren't already there
  • Show them that someone is there for them and cares about them
  • Go to a professional who can help them with their problems or diagnose them with depression

More About Teen Suicide

      Teen suicide is a major problem facing our youth. The third leading cause of death for people ages 15-24 is suicide, and in 2003, 8 percent of teenagers attempted suicide. That is one million teenagers who tried to kill themselves because they felt hopeless for life or the future. Teens feel a variety of emotions while growing up, such as stress, confusion, self-doubt, pressure to succeed, financial uncertainty, or other fears. These feelings can become overwhelming and drive individuals to think that suicide is a solution to end all of these feelings. This mindset causes the individual to become detached from their friends and family, or even become reckless in lifestyle. Eventually, they could consider suicide and attempt it. Suicidal thoughts can also be caused by depression, family struggles, questioning sexuality, or other hardships that teenagers may face in their time growing to become an adult.
